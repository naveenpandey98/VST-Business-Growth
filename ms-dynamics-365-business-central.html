2. Migration

This type of migration involves moving from an older version of Dynamics NAV (Navision) to the latest version of Dynamics 365 Business Central. Microsoft periodically releases new versions with updated features, improvements, and fixes.

Some businesses may choose to migrate from an on-premises deployment of Dynamics NAV to the cloud-based version of Dynamics 365 Business Central. This migration involves moving data and configurations from local servers to Microsoft's cloud infrastructure.

When businesses have customized their Dynamics NAV solution with specific functionalities or extensions, migrating to Business Central may require adapting or rewriting these customizations to align with the new platform.

3. Upgradation

Major version upgrades involve moving from one major release of Business Central to a newer major release. These upgrades typically include significant new features, improvements, and changes to the underlying architecture. Businesses may need to perform data migration, review customizations, and ensure compatibility with the new version.

Microsoft releases monthly updates for Business Central that include bug fixes, performance enhancements, and new features. These updates are generally incremental and do not require the same level of planning and effort as major version upgrades. However, it's still essential to monitor the release notes and test the updates in a sandbox environment before applying them to the production environment.

4. Customization / Specialist

An "indent" is a formal written request or order that is typically used in the procurement or purchasing process. It is often employed when a buyer or customer needs to procure goods or materials that are not readily available or in stock. The indent specifies the quantity, quality, and other relevant details of the items required. Indenting is common in industries where goods are sourced from suppliers or manufacturers. The process involves creating a document (the indent) that outlines the specifics of what is needed, and this document is then sent to the supplier or manufacturer for fulfilment.

In summary, an "indent" is a formalized request for goods, and the term is commonly used in procurement and supply chain management to streamline the ordering process.
